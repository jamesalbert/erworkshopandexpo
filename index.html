  • 50 minutes

    Expect the Best, Plan for the Worse - Simple precautions to keep you and your family safe.

    Speaker: Officer McBride
    my email

    Officer McBride's Bio:

    Office McBride has been in law enforcement for 9 years on patrol, as a firearms instructor, field training officer, and in investigations. He has worked closely with the FBI in joint disaster and active shooter planning. Officer McBride is a husband and father who also uses his years of training and expertise to keep his family informed of the smartest, easiest ways to worry less by staying prepared.

    Parents will walk away understanding the importance of always being alert, more specifically what to be aware of in their surroundings at home and while out. Parents will also learn ways to guard their children on the internet, on campus before and after school, and how to react during an emergency during school hours.

    Co-Parenting from Separate Houses

    Speaker: Wendy Silvers, Compassionate Parenting Educator
    my website
    my email

    Wendy Silvers' Bio:

    Wendy Silvers, ALSP, is the creator of The Empowered Parent series, a certified Echo Parenting Educator, dedicated to helping parents stay sane in an insane world and raise confident, compassionate children. She is a a Spiritual Teacher, director of the Agape Parenting Ministry, and founder of the Million Mamas Movement.

    A key contributor to conflict in co-parenting arises from ineffective communication and feeling exhausted, depleted, unheard and unappreciated. In this time together, we will explore the ways in which we can get stuck in patterns that don't serve us and create a new design for co-parenting that is empowering.

    Parents will leave with:
        ⇒ 4 keys to Co-parent peacefully
        ⇒ Strategies to unhook yourself when triggered
        ⇒ Ways to reestablish trust with co-parent
        ⇒ Tools to communicate effectively from a nonviolent, compassionate and connected place.

    Discover Your Style: Learn How You and Your Children Learn

    Speaker: Dr. Leon Campbell, Jr.
    my email

    Dr. Campbell's Bio:

    Dr. Leon Campbell, Jr. is the Director of the Agape Youth and Family Ministry at the Agape International Spiritual Center: one of the largest spiritual communities in the Los Angeles area. In his role with Agape’s youth, his goal is to draw forth the greatness in every child.

    Learn the different ways in which we learn. Every person’s brain is unique and has a method through which it “likes” to learn. We explore seven learning styles: Auditory, Visual, Kinesthetic, Verbal, Logical, Social and Solitary. We will have the opportunity to learn our own learning styles as well as how we can tailor our parenting/teaching approaches to include each and every child.

    Preserving Relationships: The Amicable Resolution of Family Challenges

    Speaker: John Adam Lazor, Esq. CFLS (Certified Family Law Specialist, State Bar of California Board of Legal Specialization)
    my website
    my email

    John Lazor's Bio:

    John and his wife, Pam, have lived in Culver City for over 17 years, and their daughters are both enrolled in the CCUSD’s Spanish Emersion Program at La Ballona Elementary School. John received his undergraduate degree in Psychology, Phi Beta Kappa with Departmental Honors and Distinction, from Stanford University, and his Juris Doctorate from the University of Michigan. He has practiced Family Law exclusively for almost 23 years, and his passion is preserving clients’ relationships with their families and friends as they resolve their family law disputes.

    This workshop focuses on the tools parents can utilize to help themselves and their children both to avoid family law conflicts and also to lessen the negative impact of the family law consequences once a conflict has begun.

    Crianza Positiva : El Cambio Que Queremos en Nuestros Hijos Empieza con Nosotros (Spanish ONLY)
    Positive Parenting: The Changes we Want in Our Children, Begin with Us)

    Adriana Sorgi
    my website
    my email

    Adriana Sorgi's Bio:

    Adriana Sorgi is a life coach with a Master's Degree in Spiritual Psychology. Her mission is to empower others to connect with their authentic- self so they can live their best life. She believes that we must connect with our JOY in order to create experiences that are in alignment with who we truly are. If we want to raise Healthy Joyful children the change must start with us Parents. The best way to teach our children is not by lecturing but by rather being our best selves. Being a good parent takes time, dedication and willingness. When our children feel loved and supported they grow up to be confident and healthy adults that thrive in life.

    En esta presentacion, los padres aprenderan:
    1) Quienes somos, cual es nuestra mision como familia.
    2) Como Crear acuerdos con tus hijos que son de beneficio mutuo padres e hijos.
    3) La differencia entre expectativas y acuerdos.
    4) Como incorporar un ritual positivo que mantiene a su familia unida en armonia.
    5) Come responder sin reaccionar.
    6) Como hacer depositos un su cuenta de banco emocional y no hacer promesas que no estamos seguros de cumplir.
    7) Como escuchar a nuestro hijos con amor y compasion.

    In this interactive Workshop, parents will learn to:
    1) How to create a mission Statement for the family.
    2) Setting healthy boundaries with our children.
    3) Expectations versus Agreements.
    4) Creating a healthy ritual that keeps the family connected in harmony.
    5) Responding versus Reacting.
    6) Making deposits in their emotional bank account.
    7) How to listen to our children from the heart
